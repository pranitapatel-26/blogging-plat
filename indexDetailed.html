The Delightful History of Gajar Halwa

Written by: Shobhan Satpathy | Published on: December 10, 2024

Gajar Halwa, also known as Gajrela, is a traditional Indian dessert that has graced our tables for centuries. Originating from the Mughal kitchens, this rich, creamy dessert was inspired by Persian and Middle Eastern cuisines. Over time, it became a staple during Indian festivals like Diwali and weddings.

The star ingredient, gajar (carrots), is slow-cooked with milk, ghee, and sugar, creating a melt-in-your-mouth experience. The addition of cardamom, nuts, and raisins enhances its taste and aroma. Gajar Halwa is not just a dessert; it's a symbol of celebration and warmth, served with love in every spoonful.
